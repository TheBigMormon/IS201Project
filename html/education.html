Education

"You must get all of the education that you possibly can."
- Gordon B. Hinckley

Graduating from Rancho Bernardo High School was one of the first great accomplishments in my life. I played 3 varsity sports from 10th-12th grade while maintaining a minimun GPA of 4.0. I learned how to use my time effectively and efficiently as there was not often downtime in my schedule. Each morning at 6am I also attended a religious class (seminary) where I enhanced my spirituality as well. My senior year I was one of 4 students left with 100% attendance in a class that started with 25 people my freshman year and slowly faded to 12-15 people senior year.

While a senior in high school I also enrolled myself in a class at the local community college.(Palomar Community College) There I was able to get ahead in my college education These educational steps allowed me an opporunity, which I took, to attend Brigham Young University.(BYU) I have now completed 5 semesters at BYU and am a sophomore.

I love mathematics and hope to earn my Masters degree in college after serving a 2 year mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in Santiago, Dominican Republic. I am debating about what I will major in, but because of my abilities in math I hope to go into finance, accounting, or business management/administration.
